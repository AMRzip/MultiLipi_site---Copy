Frequently Asked

Frequently Asked Questions

How many languages can I translate my website into?

Translate your website into more than 22 Indian languages, including custom languages such as Hindi, Tamil, Gujrati, Marathi

What is a language subdomain/subdirectory?

If for example, your original website is www.example.com, a subdirectory would be www.example.com/hi/ (for the Hindi version), and a subdomain would be www.hi.example.com, and so on.

How is MultiLipi optimized for multilingual SEO?

MultiLipi follows Google’s best practices for multilingual SEO. When launching a multilingual website you also need to implement technical elements such as hreflang tags and create language subdirectories or subdomains. Both of these aspects are automatically handled by MultiLipi as well as translating your metadata, an important part of your multilingual SEO.

How accurate is machine translation?

We leverage the power of AI and neural machine translation from the best translation, to give the most accurate translation return. You then have full editing control through your MultiLipi Dashboard.

How can I edit machine translations?

You can edit your machine translations through your MultiLipi Dashboard. Edit the translations yourself or add your own translators to make quick edits to any translation displayed on your website.

Where do my translations exist?

Your translations are stored by MultiLipi, accessible through your MultiLipi Dashboard. This gives you an easier overview of all your translations through a simple interface.

How many translated words do I need?

Translated words indicate the number of words you can translate within your MultiLipi plan. To know the number of translated words you need, you must first know how many words you have on your website and how many languages you wish to translate your website into. You can learn the total number of words on your website using our Word Count Tool and easily get a plan suggestion based on your needs.

Can I use MultiLipi on more than one website?

Yes, if you have at least a Pro plan you have the multisite feature. It allows you to manage several websites separately and gives access to one person per website.
